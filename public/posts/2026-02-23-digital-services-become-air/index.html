The Shift: When Intelligence Becomes a Commodity#

Mustafa Suleyman, Microsoft’s AI chief, made a stunning prediction: “Most, if not every, task in white-collar fields will be automated by AI within 18 months.”

That’s not a distant forecast. That’s early 2027.

And he’s not alone. Ford CEO Jim Farley: “AI will replace literally half of all white-collar workers.” Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei: “AI could wipe out half of all entry-level white-collar jobs.”

Here’s what’s happeningâ€”and more importantly, where the value is going.

The Paradox: Abundance Kills Value#

What Happens When Services Become Air#

Air is essential. But air is free.

The same paradox is hitting non-physical services:

ServiceBefore AIAfter AI
Writing$50-200/articleNear-zero marginal cost
Translation$0.10-0.20/wordNear-free
Data analysis$100-500/reportAutomated in minutes
Legal research$300-1000/caseInstant retrieval
Coding$50-150/hour10x faster with AI

The pattern is clear: When AI can produce something, its price collapses.

But here’s the twist most people miss:

Value doesn’t disappear. It migrates.

  • 2025: University graduate unemployment at 5.8% (up 30% from 2022)
  • 2025: 55,000 job cuts explicitly attributed to AI

The structural shift:

  • For 200 years, technology replaced muscle labor
  • Now, technology is replacing brain labor
  • The “Luddite Fallacy”â€”that technology always creates more jobs than it destroysâ€”faces its ultimate test

The White-Collar Crisis: Deeper Than We Think#

Entry-Level Jobs: The First Casualty#

The entry-level white-collar job is the canary in the coal mine.

Why? Because entry-level work is:

  • Most repetitive
  • Most rule-based
  • Lowest stakes if automated

Investment Implications: Where to Bet#

The Counterintuitive Opportunity#

If white-collar services are becoming abundant, where should investors look?

Toward physical scarcity.

Thesis: Dow Jones Companies as the New Value Play#

The Dow Jones Industrial Average represents 30 of America’s most established companies. Many are “boring” industrial, financial, and consumer giants.

Why they’re positioned to win:

Financial (JPMorgan, Goldman, AmEx)AI automates analysis, cuts costs
Consumer (P&G, Coca-Cola, McDonald’s)AI optimizes supply chains, marketing

The Bottom Line#

The AI revolution is creating a paradox:

Digital services are becoming abundant.
Physical services are becoming scarce.
Value migrates from the former to the latter.

The winners won’t be the companies making AI. They’ll be the companies:

  1. Using AI to improve physical operations
  2. Owning assets that can’t be digitized
  3. Serving markets where AI increases demand for human oversight

The losers will be the companies whose core productâ€”non-physical servicesâ€”is being commoditized.

The question isn’t whether AI will transform the economy. The question is: are you positioned where value is going, or where it’s leaving?

When services become air, value doesn’t disappear.
It flows to the physical world AI can’t touch.
